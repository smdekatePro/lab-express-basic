Biography

Sir Thomas Sean Connery (1930 – 2020) was a Scottish actor who became world-famous for his role as the first cinematic James Bond. He starred in seven Bond films between 1962 and 1983, beginning with Dr. No.

Connery’s rugged charm, sophistication, and wit made him the definitive Bond in the eyes of many fans. Beyond Bond, he had an illustrious acting career, winning an Academy Award, two BAFTA Awards, and three Golden Globes.

In 2000, he was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for his services to film drama.
